The History of Pizzas

The delicacy of pizzas were originated in the country of Italy around the 10th century A.D. It soon spread worlwide and is now one of the most common delicacies in the world. The two biggest pizza chains currently are Pizza Hut and Dominos. It started to gain popularity around the 17th century, and was brought to the United States in around the late ninteeth century. The first pizzeria in the United States was Lombardi's, which was opened in 1905.

Pizza is prepared by baking it in an oven. After it is baked, a variety of toppings, such as olives and pepporoni are added to make it took more scrumptous and attractive. The bottom layer of the pizza is called its crust. It is traditionally plain, but sometimes is prepared using garlic, herbs or is stuffed with cheese. Doughs of pizzas often contain sugars, both to help its yeast rise and enhance browning of the crust.

Cheese is the main and the most delicious ingredient in a pizza, with mozeralla cheese being the most popular. The highest quality buffalo mozeralla is produced in the origin town of Naples, Italy. In 1997, it was estimated that annual production of pizza cheese was 1 million tonnes (1,100,000 short tons) in the U.S. and 100,000 tonnes (110,000 short tons) in Europe.
